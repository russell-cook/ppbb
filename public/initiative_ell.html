Description (Summary)

During the 77th Legislative Session, both the Governor and Legislature agreed that providing funding to better serve the English Language Learner population, particularly in the early years of education (pre-kindergarten through fourth grade), was critical to improving the educational success of Nevada’s children. Our vision for Nevada’s English Language Learners is that students acquire academic English and content mastery when they are meaningfully engaged in undiluted grade level content learning and problem solving with their teachers and their peers. ELL and former ELL students are successful when they graduate with the ability to continue in post-secondary education or training opportunities. We expect they will have the foundation and disposition to participate in and contribute to the society of their home and community.
Expanded Description >>

Justification (Summary)

The ELL population has not previously received dedicated state education funds, yet over 68,000 of the state’s 445,000 students, or almost one out of every six students, is an ELL student. Current and former ELL students comprise nearly one of every three Nevada students. However, data suggests that students who successfully transition from being an ELL student to a former ELL student (former ELL students are those who have passed the English proficiency examination), within the expected timeframe (usually four to six years) perform better on assessments than the general student population. Students within two years of exit perform 4.1% higher on the Math CRT and 1.8% higher on the Language Arts CRT.
Expanded Justification >>


This is a new program requiring $24,950,000 in new funding for each year of the 2014-15 biennium. In addition, the Initiative requires $50,000 per year for costs associated with the English Master Council (SB 504, Section 1.4).
