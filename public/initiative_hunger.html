Initiative: Hunger Reduction

Summary

Details

DHHS is proposing an investment of an additional $2.3 million annually to reduce hunger in Nevada. This program will provide additional subgrants to the network of community providers with the goal of over 90% of allocated funds used directly to feed needy Nevadans and to educate Nevadan’s regarding healthy eating habits.

Funding

Lead Agency: DHHS - Welfare Division
Secondary Agencies: Dept of Education, Dept of Agriculture

Cost Summary

$2.3 million in Tobacco funds each year of the upcoming biennium. This is a 40% increase in direct funding provided by the State as current funding provided using a combination of state and federal funds is approx. $5.75 million annually. There is currently no ability to further leverage Federal funds through this investment. We can leverage private funding sources with approximately a 50% match to additional state funds – or $1.15 million per fiscal year.

Population/Caseload

Population Served

Food supply programs are targeted directly to populations with less than 135% of the Federal Poverty Rate. Some resources are available to individuals with incomes up to 150% based upon specific criteria. Education programs are targeted to populations receiving any support services through the state, local and non-profit resources. Other education funds will be targeted directly to school age children K through 5th grade.

Indirect Population Served

Addressing food insecurity and healthy eating habits has indirect benefits to the community at large. Individuals with adequate food are less likely to commit crimes, have better medical outcomes and require fewer other social support services.
